# Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
## Course: CSE0001 - Digital Literacy

---

## DO's ✅

1. Keep your LinkedIn profile updated and professional
   at all times - add courses, skills, and achievements
   as you complete them.

2. Think before you post - ask yourself "would I be
   comfortable if my professor or future employer saw this?"

3. Use privacy settings on platforms like Instagram
   and Facebook to control who can see your content.

4. Engage positively online - share useful resources,
   congratulate peers, and contribute meaningfully
   to discussions.

5. Use your college email ID for signing up on
   academic platforms like GitHub, Kaggle, and LinkedIn.

---

## DON'Ts ❌

1. Never share personal information like your home
   address, phone number, or daily routine publicly
   on social media.

2. Do not post negative comments about your college,
   faculty, or classmates on any public platform.

3. Do not accept friend or connection requests from
   people you do not know personally.

4. Never share unverified news, rumours, or forwards
   without checking the source first.

5. Do not use the same password for multiple platforms
   - if one account is hacked, all others become vulnerable.

---

## Quick Rule to Remember
If you would not say it in a classroom,
do not post it online.
